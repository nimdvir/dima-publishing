## Assessment Design Notes

This RAT supports readiness assessment for Chapter 2: Foundations of Information Systems. The questions are drawn from the chapter's four-part arc — Data Meaning (DIKW, R.E.A.D., data quality), Business Performance (IPO, efficiency, effectiveness, KPIs), Information Systems as Engines (IS vs IT, five components, information behavior, operating loop), and Managing for Value (MIS, BITM, strategic alignment, governance). The Let's Build course-as-a-system framing and the chapter's online-retailer running example inform the scenario-based questions.

### AI-Resistance Strategies Used

1. **Chapter-specific reasoning** — questions require the chapter's exact frameworks (DIKW guiding questions, R.E.A.D. stage names, five-component names, five-part operating loop).
2. **Schema-specific context** — questions reference the chapter's IT/IS/MIS/BITM comparison table and the five-part operating loop.
3. **Scenario stems with embedded traps** — CRM failure and KPI distortion scenarios require distinguishing surface symptoms from root causes.
4. **Multi-answer options requiring fine-grained discrimination** — Select ALL questions include distractors from adjacent concepts.
5. **Non-obvious correct answers paraphrased** — Q9 and Q31 paraphrase chapter arguments rather than using keyword-matched definitions.
6. **Stage-progression reasoning** — Q17 and Q30 require identifying correct stages in R.E.A.D. and data-to-performance chains.
7. **Distractors from adjacent but distinct concepts** — Q2 includes "Reporting and Retrieval" and "Records and Relationships" as near-miss distractors.
