# book platform

1. The home screen contain the book title, author name, and a brief description of the book's purpose and target audience + cover
2. The home screen contains a login option. Students login with their netid and student number, which are verified against a csv or database (they are taken from the course registration). Instructors and guests login with their netid and a password, which are verified against a secure instructor database.
3. If the student haven't paid, they will be redirect to a payment page where they can pay with credit card or paypal. After payment, they will be redirected back to the home screen or page they were on and their access will be updated in the database.
4. The book is organized into chapters and sections, with a clear navigation menu that allows users to easily find and access the content they need.
5. The side bar is collapsible based on chapters and subsections, with clear labels and icons to indicate the content of each section.
6. Each chapter] contains the main page, let'sbuils section, term-tresury section, reflection questions section and RAT section. Each section is clearly labeled and accessible from the chapter page.
7. The Labs are at the end of the book in their own dedicated section, and refrence the chapters they corralate with. Each lab contains clear instructions, sample data, and any necessary code snippets or templates to help students complete the exercises.
8. The chapters also refrence the labs that corralate with them, with clear links and descriptions of the lab exercises.
9. There is a progress tracker that allows students to see which chapters and sections they have completed, and which ones they still need to work on. This can be a simple checklist or a more visual progress bar.
10. The progress tracker is updated automatically as students complete chapters and sections, and can also be manually updated by students if they need to mark something as complete.
11. The progress is also reported to the instructor dashboard, so instructors can see how students are progressing through the book and identify any areas where students may be struggling.
12. The main section of each chapter is organized into clear subsections with headings and subheadings, and includes a mix of text, images, and examples to help students understand the material. However, it arrives as one file. 
13. The reader include arrows at the bottom to indicate what is the next and previous section or subsection.
14. I want to be able to share links to specific pages in the book. when the users click on them, they will prompt for login if they are not logged in, and then take them to the specific page after login.
