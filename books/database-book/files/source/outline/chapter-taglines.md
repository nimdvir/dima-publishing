# Student-Facing Chapter Learning Sentences

## Chapter 1: Introduction to the Textbook

Students will learn how the course is organized around the journey from raw data to business decisions. They will also learn how the R.E.A.D. framework and the Grading Database project will guide their work throughout the semester.

## Chapter 2: Foundations of Information Systems

Students will learn how information systems combine people, processes, data, software, and hardware to support business performance. They will also learn how raw data becomes useful information, knowledge, and managerial insight.

## Chapter 3: Understanding Data Fundamentals

Students will learn what data is, how it can be classified, and why data quality matters before any database or analysis can be trusted. They will also learn how metadata, governance, ethics, and the data lifecycle shape responsible data use.

## Chapter 4: Introduction to Databases

Students will learn why spreadsheets are limited for managing organizational data and how databases provide a more reliable structure. They will also learn the basic building blocks of databases, including tables, keys, constraints, and database management systems.

## Chapter 5: SQL — The Language of Databases

Students will learn how to use SQL to retrieve, filter, sort, join, and summarize data from relational databases. They will also learn how SQL helps turn stored data into answers to specific business questions.

## Chapter 6: The Relational Model

Students will learn how relational databases organize data into connected tables to reduce redundancy and improve accuracy. They will also learn how entities, attributes, relationships, primary keys, foreign keys, and referential integrity work together.

## Chapter 7: Data Normalization

Students will learn how to improve database design by identifying redundancy, dependency problems, and design anomalies. They will also learn how 1NF, 2NF, and 3NF help create cleaner, more reliable relational databases.

## Chapter 8: Midterm Review — Concepts

Students will learn how the first half of the course fits together as one connected framework. They will review data fundamentals, databases, SQL, the relational model, and normalization in preparation for the midterm.

## Chapter 9: Database Design and ER Modeling

Students will learn how to translate business requirements into database designs before implementation begins. They will also learn how ERDs, logical design, and normalization help create systems that support accurate querying and reporting. Designing before writing advanced queries gives students the structural foundation they need for the deeper SQL work in Chapter 10.

## Chapter 10: Advanced SQL for Business Analysis

Students will learn how to write more powerful SQL queries for deeper analysis, including subqueries, CTEs, CASE expressions, and window functions. Building on the design skills from Chapter 9, they will also learn how advanced SQL patterns can support performance measurement and business intelligence.

## Chapter 11: Database Administration

Students will learn how databases are secured, maintained, backed up, and optimized after they are built. They will also learn why database administration is essential for business continuity, compliance, and operational reliability.

## Chapter 12: Business Intelligence and Analytics for Performance Improvement

Students will learn how organizations use databases to produce reports, dashboards, KPIs, and managerial insights. They will also learn the basics of data warehouses, ETL, star schemas, OLAP, and performance measurement.

## Chapter 13: Advanced Database Techniques

Students will learn how advanced database features improve performance, automation, security, and scalability. They will also learn when to use indexes, views, stored procedures, triggers, and performance monitoring tools.

## Chapter 14: Power BI — Data Visualization and Business Reporting

Students will learn how to turn database output into visual reports and interactive dashboards using Power BI. They will also learn how to design reports that communicate insights clearly to managers and decision-makers.

## Chapter 15: Business Strategy and Information Systems

Students will learn how information systems, databases, analytics, and governance support business strategy, and how technology can create competitive advantage or risk when poorly aligned with organizational goals. The chapter closes with an epilogue, **Designing Systems That Matter**, that connects technical skill to ethical responsibility, data stewardship, managerial judgment, and lifelong learning in a data-driven world. This closing section grows directly out of the strategy material rather than standing apart from it.

## Chapter 16: Final Review and Course Integration

Students will learn how to connect all major course concepts into a complete data-to-decision framework. They will also apply what they have learned to realistic business scenarios and reflect on how these skills support future careers.
