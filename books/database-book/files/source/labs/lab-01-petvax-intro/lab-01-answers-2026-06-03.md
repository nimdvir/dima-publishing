## Overview

Lab 01 is an orientation **quiz-only** lab. There is no submitted file and no artifact rubric. Score the ten quiz questions below. Questions 2 and 4 award partial credit as described in the notes.

## Quiz Answer Key

| Q | Type | Correct Answer | Points | Rationale |
| - | ---- | -------------- | ------ | --------- |
| 1 | Multiple choice | **B** | 1 | The database organizes operational records so staff can run the business and answer questions; it does not replace staff or store unrelated data. |
| 2 | Select all | **A, B, C, D, E** | 2 | All are realistic clinic data except F (the clinic's favorite color), which is not operational data. |
| 3 | Multiple choice | **B** | 1 | "Which owners still have unpaid invoices?" is an operational/managerial question the data can answer. The others are unrelated to clinic records. |
| 4 | Matching | **1-C, 2-D, 3-A, 4-E, 5-B** | 2 | Owner = the responsible person; Pet = the animal; Visit = an encounter; Treatment = a service performed; Invoice = a billing record. |
| 5 | Multiple choice | **B** | 1 | One large spreadsheet encourages duplication and inconsistency, is hard to update reliably, and weakens reporting. |
| 6 | Multiple choice | **B** | 1 | One owner to many pets and one pet to many visits are natural one-to-many relationships that connected tables represent. |
| 7 | Multiple choice | **A** | 1 | The Grading Database is the guided teaching case; PetVax is the applied transfer case. |
| 8 | Multiple choice | **B** | 1 | Asking what questions the data should answer is the analyst role. |
| 9 | Multiple choice | **A** | 1 | The course uses spreadsheets, Access, SQLite, PostgreSQL/Supabase, diagramming tools, and GitHub. |
| 10 | Multiple choice | **C** | 1 | Lab 01 is quiz-only; there is no file to submit. |

**Total: 12 points.**

## Scoring Notes

- **Question 2 (Select all, 2 pts):** Award full credit only when all of A, B, C, D, E are selected and F is not. Award 1 point if the student selects four of the five correct options with no incorrect selections. Selecting F caps the score at 1 point.
- **Question 4 (Matching, 2 pts):** Award 2 points for all five pairs correct, 1 point for three or four correct, 0 otherwise.
- All other questions are 1 point, all-or-nothing.

## Notes for the Instructor

- This lab confirms orientation understanding only. There is no artifact file and no AI-grader rubric for Lab 01.
- Beginning with Lab 02, labs add a submitted artifact file graded against a rubric, alongside the quiz.
