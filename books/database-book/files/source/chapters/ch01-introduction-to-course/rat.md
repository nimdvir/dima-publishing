## Assessment Design Notes

This RAT checks that students completed Chapter 1 and can reason with the book's orientation before class. It emphasizes the data-to-decisions arc, the five core competencies, the Tool Ecosystem, the two running projects, the chapter rhythm, Bloom's Taxonomy, the GD/GDB preview, PetVax transfer practice, and the success habits that support technical business learning.

Because Chapter 1 is an orientation chapter, it previews GD/GDB entities, SQL tasks, and platform roles but does not yet ask students to execute SQL or inspect a full schema. The RAT therefore grounds schema and SQL readiness in the Let's Build preview: students, class sessions, deliverables, individual scores, attendance records, grade retrieval, attendance percentages, missing scores, minimum final grades, and letter-grade mapping.

**Bloom Distribution**

| Bloom Level | Required Count | Intent                                                                |
| ----------- | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Remember    | 8              | Foundational vocabulary and structural facts with minimal pure recall |
| Understand  | 8              | Explain why concepts matter, interpret, and paraphrase                |
| Apply       | 8              | Use chapter concepts in realistic scenarios and technical tasks       |
| Analyze     | 8              | Compare alternatives, break down systems, and diagnose trade-offs     |
| Evaluate    | 8              | Judge design quality and choose the best approach under constraints   |

**Design Criterion Coverage**

The design criteria below identify the strongest examples for each target category. They are not intended to label every question because several Chapter 1 questions bridge concept recognition and application readiness.

| Design Criterion  | Bloom Sections Used                  | Questions                                              | Count |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | ----- |
| Application-based | Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate | 11, 21, 22, 23, 27, 28, 30, 31, 32, 36, 37, 38, 39, 40 | 14    |
| Scenario-based    | Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate | 13, 17, 18, 19, 20, 24, 25, 26, 29, 33, 34, 35         | 12    |
| Definition-only   | Remember, Understand                 | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12, 16                         | 10    |

**AI-Resistance Strategies Used**

- Chapter-specific reasoning over generic knowledge: questions use the Chapter 1 arc, competencies, projects, rhythm, and source examples.
- Schema-specific context where Chapter 1 allows it: questions reference GD/GDB entities and tasks such as deliverables, attendance, quiz averages, missing scores, and letter-grade mapping.
- Scenario stems with embedded traps: coffee shop, clinic, hospital, retailer, and analyst scenarios require applying chapter logic, not generic business sense.
- Multi-answer options requiring fine-grained discrimination: Select ALL questions mix real chapter terms with plausible distractors.
- Distractors drawn from adjacent but distinct concepts: options contrast tools, workflow stages, Bloom levels, companion sections, and core competencies.
- Stage-progression reasoning: questions use the data-to-decisions arc, chapter rhythm, six-part course sequence, and GD/GDB-to-PetVax transfer path.
- Platform-specific details: questions distinguish Access, SQLite, Supabase / PostgreSQL, BigQuery, Power BI, Google Forms, Lucidchart, GitHub, and AI-supported workflows.
