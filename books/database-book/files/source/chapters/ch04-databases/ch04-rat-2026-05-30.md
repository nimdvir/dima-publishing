## Assessment Design Notes

This RAT checks whether students completed Chapter 4 and can reason with its central movement from spreadsheets and file environments to structured database systems. It prepares students for Chapter 5 SQL work and Chapter 6 relational design by emphasizing tables, keys, constraints, DBMS layers, platform trade-offs, and the Grading Database/PetVax examples.

| Bloom Level | Required Count | Intent |
| ----------- | -------------- | ------ |
| Remember | 8 | Foundational vocabulary and structural facts with minimal pure recall |
| Understand | 8 | Explain why concepts matter, interpret, and paraphrase |
| Apply | 8 | Use chapter concepts in realistic scenarios and technical tasks |
| Analyze | 8 | Compare alternatives, break down systems, and diagnose trade-offs |
| Evaluate | 8 | Judge design quality and choose the best approach under constraints |

| Design Criterion | Bloom Sections Used | Questions | Count |
| ---------------- | ------------------- | --------- | ----- |
| Application-based | Apply, Analyze, Evaluate | 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 26, 28, 31, 34, 36, 40 | 14 |
| Scenario-based | Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate | 11, 20, 24, 25, 27, 29, 33, 35, 37, 38, 39, 40 | 12 |
| Definition-only | Remember, Understand | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12, 15 | 10 |

AI-resistance strategies used:

- Chapter-specific reasoning over generic database vocabulary.
- Schema-specific context using `STUDENT`, `STUDENT_GRADE`, `GRADEBOOK`, `GRADE_WEIGHT`, `StudentID`, `Email`, `Score`, and `DeliverableType`.
- Scenario stems from the Grading Database, PetVax, Access Let's Build, and business file-silo examples.
- Multi-answer questions requiring discrimination between nearby concepts such as redundancy, inconsistency, constraints, keys, and views.
- Distractors drawn from adjacent but distinct concepts, such as reports versus tables, SQL versus DBMS, and logical versus physical view.
- Platform-specific details for Access, SQLite, and PostgreSQL/Supabase.
- Stage-progression reasoning that prepares students for Chapter 5 SQL and Chapter 6 relational design.
