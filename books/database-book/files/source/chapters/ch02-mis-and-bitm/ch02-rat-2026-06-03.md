## Assessment Design Notes

This RAT measures readiness for Chapter 2: Foundations of Information Systems. Questions span vocabulary, frameworks, applied reasoning, and evaluation of system design choices. The RAT rewards students who read the chapter carefully and can connect concepts to the chapter's examples — not students who memorize definitions in isolation.

### Bloom Distribution

| Bloom Level | Questions | Count |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Remember | 1–8 | 8 |
| Understand | 9–16 | 8 |
| Apply | 17–24 | 8 |
| Analyze | 25–32 | 8 |
| Evaluate | 33–40 | 8 |

### Design Criterion Coverage

| Design Criterion | Bloom Sections Used | Questions | Count |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Application-based | Apply, Analyze, Evaluate | 17–24, 25–32, 33–40 | 14 |
| Scenario-based | Understand, Apply, Analyze | 9, 11, 19, 21, 23, 27, 29, 31 | 10 |
| Definition-only | Remember, Understand | 1–8, 10, 12–16 | 14 |

### AI-Resistance Strategies Used

1. **Chapter-specific reasoning** — Questions reference chapter examples (online retailer, grading database, hospital, coffee shop) rather than generic business scenarios.
2. **Schema-specific context** — Select ALL questions require discriminating among the chapter's exact frameworks (DIKW vs R.E.A.D., IS vs IT vs MIS vs BITM).
3. **Scenario stems with embedded traps** — Application scenarios include distractors that sound plausible but violate a specific chapter principle (e.g., confusing IT with IS, or efficiency with effectiveness).
4. **Multi-answer options requiring fine-grained discrimination** — Select ALL stems use closely related chapter concepts as distractors (five-component framework, R.E.A.D. stages, IPO elements).
5. **Distractors drawn from adjacent but distinct concepts** — Wrong answers reference real chapter terms used in a subtly wrong way (IPO model for IS, KPI for data quality).
6. **Non-obvious correct answers paraphrased rather than keyword-matched** — Key definitions are restated in alternate wording.
7. **Stage-progression reasoning** — Several questions require placing a scenario at the correct DIKW level or R.E.A.D. stage.
